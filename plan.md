- **What this covers:** 70-year history of AI—why now is different for developers

## 1:00–3:00 | The Early Days of AI (1950s–1970s)

> _[Scroll down and expand the "Foundation & Early Optimism" era]_

- **1950 - Turing Test:** Can machines think? Becomes benchmark for machine intelligence
- **1956 - Dartmouth Workshop:** AI formally established as field; predictions of AGI within 20 years
- **The approach:** Encode human knowledge as rules → IF/THEN logic → feed to computer
- **1997 - Deep Blue vs. Kasparov:** Proof-of-concept works, but only at chess; can't learn or adapt
- **Parallel track - Neural Networks:** Rosenblatt's Perceptron (1957) promises learning from data
  - > _[Find "The Perceptron" entry]_
- **Initial promise:** Neural networks showed potential for learning from data
- **Setback:** Progress halted

---

## 3:00–4:30 | The AI Winters (1970s–1980s, Late 1980s–1990s)

> _[Scroll to find and expand the "The First Winter" era]_

- **Two long winters:** Progress halted twice (1970s, late 1980s)
- **Why:** Rule-based systems brittle and fragile; neural networks couldn't learn complex patterns
- **1969 - Perceptrons book:** Minsky & Papert prove single-layer nets can't solve key problems → kills research for 15 years
  - > _[Show "Perceptrons Book Published"]_
- **Market collapse:** ALPAC Report, Lighthill Report, Lisp machine crash → funding dries up
- **What was missing:**
  - Computing power (silicon too slow)
  - Data (internet didn't exist)
  - Algorithms (no way to train deep nets)
- **Result:** Field goes quiet for nearly 20 years; researchers leave the field

---

## 4:30–6:30 | Machine Learning Transforms Everything (1990s–2010s)

> _[Scroll down and expand "The Quiet Revolution" era]_

- **Paradigm shift:** Stop asking "encode knowledge as rules" → start asking "learn from data"
- **This is machine learning:** Systems improve from examples, not explicit programming
- **1986 - Backpropagation:** Missing piece found—how to train multi-layer neural networks
  - > _[Find "Learning Representations by Back-Propagating Errors"]_
  - Takes years for field to embrace it
- **1990s:** Machine learning goes mainstream
  - Email filters, recommendation systems, credit scoring
  - Practical, not perfect
- **2006 - Deep Belief Nets:** Geoffrey Hinton demonstrates training deep networks layer-by-layer
  - > _[Find "A Fast Learning Algorithm for Deep Belief Nets"]_
  - Neural networks become viable again
- **2009 - ImageNet:** Fei-Fei Li creates massive labeled image database
  - Perfect playground for researchers
  - > _[Find "ImageNet" entry]_
- **2012 - AlexNet wins:** University of Toronto cuts error rate in half with deep learning
  - > _[Scroll to 2012 and find "AlexNet Wins ImageNet"]_
  - Triggers widespread adoption of neural networks

---

## 6:30–8:30 | Deep Learning Breakthroughs (2012–2017)

> _[Expand "The Deep Learning Era" and highlight featured events]_

- **Deep learning:** Machine learning with many layers; each layer learns more complex patterns
  - Bottom layers: edges and shapes
  - Top layers: recognize objects like "dog" or "cat"
- **2011 - IBM Watson wins Jeopardy!:** Defeats human champions; understands natural language
  - > _[Find and highlight "IBM Watson Wins Jeopardy!"]_
- **2016 - AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol:** Combines deep learning with reinforcement learning
  - > _[Find "AlphaGo Defeats Lee Sedol"]_
  - Defeats world champion at complex strategic game
- **2017 - Attention Is All You Need:** New architecture called **Transformer** revolutionizes language understanding
  - > _[Find "Attention Is All You Need"]_
  - Becomes foundation for all modern language models
- **2018-2020:** GPT, GPT-2, GPT-3 released
  - > _[Scroll to show GPT progression]_
  - GPT-3 can write essays, code, poetry
  - **But:** Public doesn't know about it yet; still in research labs
- **2017-2020 - Prerequisites met:** Sufficient compute power, data availability, and architectural improvements in place

## 8:30–10:00 | The Recent Boom (2022–Present)

> _[Scroll to 2022 and expand "The Generative AI Boom" era]_

- **November 2022 - ChatGPT launches:** Free public website interface
  - > _[Find and highlight "ChatGPT"]_
  - Reaches 100M users in 2 months
- **Why adoption accelerated:** Three factors:
  1. **Quality:** Significant improvement in output quality and conversational ability
  2. **Accessibility:** Simple web interface, no technical expertise required
  3. **Practical applications:** Demonstrated value for real business tasks
- **2023-2024 - Multiple releases:**
  - Stable Diffusion (text-to-image)
  - > _[Scroll through 2023-2024 featured events]_
  - Google Gemini, Meta LLaMA
  - Claude, Gato, and other models
- **2024 - Recognition:** Nobel Prizes awarded to pioneers of deep learning
  - > _[Find "2024 Nobel Prizes for AI"]_
  - Field elevated to highest scientific recognition
- **Now (2026):** Current state of AI adoption and deployment
